Story behind the name

In 1955, our proud grandfather, Venkitaraman, set foot on Burma's foreign shores, lured by its whispered promises. With boundless optimism, he established the Indian Diary Farm, a sanctuary for his dreams, and fortune smiled upon him. But fate's treacherous tide turned when civil war tore through the land.

Forced to abandon his cherished creation, Venkitaraman returned to Calicut, stripped of possessions but resolute in spirit. Undeterred, he embraced a fresh start, transforming adversity into opportunity.

In 1965, in the bustling streets of Calicut, he breathed life into a new endeavor: Burma Metals. Its name a tribute to the courage and confidence instilled in him by Burma.

Stroke by stroke, his skilled hands fashioned utensils infused with indomitable bravery.

Thus, ‘Burma Metals’ became a symbol of unwavering determination, a testament to his triumphant spirit. As time passed, his story endured, etched upon each metallic surface, a reminder of the strength within us all.

Venkitaraman's odyssey stands as a lasting testament to the human spirit, a beacon of hope emerging from the depths of despair. May his legacy continue to inspire generations, as they grasp the echoes of grasp the echoes of his extraordinary journey.
